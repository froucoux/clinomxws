  • 2026

    Jan 2026

    Publication

    MIE 2026 — Clinical Diagnosis of Rare Diseases using Leaky Noisy-OR Bayesian Networks

    Medical Informatics Europe (MIE) 2026

    Paper by F. Roucoux and S. Jodogne (UCLouvain / ICTEAM) submitted to MIE 2026. Presents a probabilistic method for rare disease diagnosis using leaky noisy-OR Bayesian networks automatically constructed from Orphanet and HPO data. The model contains over 8,000 diseases and 9,000 phenotype variables, and achieves 56.2% Top-3 diagnostic accuracy on 384 real clinical cases, competitive with state-of-the-art tools. Released as free and open-source software.

    EEG Data Integration Strategy Defined

    UCLouvain — ICTEAM

    Following participation in the COST CA24161 Management Committee, a strategy for EEG data integration into CLINOM-X was defined, adopting DICOM WG-32 and BIDS standards, aligning metadata with COST recommendations, and leveraging the Orthanc ecosystem for EEG data addressing from OpenMRS.

    Milestone

    Rare Disease Diagnostic Pipeline — Submitted to MIE 2026

    UCLouvain — ICTEAM

    A rare disease diagnostic pipeline was developed, leveraging HPO/Orphanet annotations within a Noisy-OR probabilistic model. Evaluated on 300+ clinical cases, it achieves a Top-3 accuracy of 56.2%, competitive with current state-of-the-art tools. The work has been submitted to MIE 2026.

  • 2025

    Jul 2025

    Milestone

    OpenMRS Confirmed as Technological Foundation

    UCLouvain — ICTEAM

    OpenMRS was confirmed as the technological foundation for electronic data collection, offering concept-oriented modelling, configurable forms, and REST/FHIR interfaces. A Java REST client is under development, already supporting full management of core resources (Patients, Visits, Encounters, Observations, Concepts). FHIR integration strategy defined: QuestionnaireResponses stored as JSON within OpenMRS Observations, and Questionnaires as Concepts, with UUID-based alignment.

  • 2025

    Jan 2025

    Milestone

    Platform Analysis Phase — Core Requirements Identified

    UCLouvain — ICTEAM

    Analysis phase for an open, standards-based platform for managing clinical, medico-technical, and omic data in clinical research. Core requirements identified: user and role management, research project tracking, data collection instruments (using a two-level archetype/template model), dataset management with open metadata, authorised access and visualisation, import/export in CSV, Avro, Parquet, FHIR JSON, and OMOP formats, and a REST API for external integration.
